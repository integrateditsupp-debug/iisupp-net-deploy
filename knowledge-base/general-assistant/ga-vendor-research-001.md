# Vendor / seller research

## 1. Quick checks (5 minutes)
- Search "**company-name reviews**", "**company-name complaints**", "**company-name reddit**".
- Trustpilot / Sitejabber / BBB rating.
- Check social profiles — are they active, do they engage with negative reviews?
- Search business name on the state Secretary of State business search (US) — is it registered?
- Map the address — is it a real building or a residential / fake address?

## 2. Deep checks (for big purchases)
- Domain age via whois.
- LinkedIn — do real employees show up? How old are profiles?
- Press / news coverage — is the company referenced anywhere reputable?
- Court records / lawsuits if material.
- Better Business Bureau accreditation (note: BBB accreditation is paid; absence isn't decisive).

## 3. Reputation scoring (rough)
- **Trust:** longstanding, mainstream press coverage, lots of consistent reviews, real address, real employees on LinkedIn.
- **Caution:** newer, fewer reviews, single founder, unclear contact info.
- **Decline:** young domain, no real address, no online footprint, complaints of non-delivery.

## 4. ARIA's framing
- "Here's what I see about [vendor]: [summary]. Looks reputable / mixed / I'd be cautious."
- Never assert "definitely scam" without strong evidence.
- Offer alternatives when caution warranted.
