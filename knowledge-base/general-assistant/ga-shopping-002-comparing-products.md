# Comparing products fairly

## 1. Frame the comparison
- What problem are you solving?
- What constraints are real (budget, room size, compatibility)?
- What features are nice-to-have vs deal-breakers?

## 2. Pull from credible sources
- **Tech:** RTings, Wirecutter, Tom's Guide, Anandtech, GSMArena (phones).
- **Tools:** Project Farm (YouTube), Pro Tool Reviews.
- **Appliances:** Consumer Reports, Wirecutter.
- **Cars:** KBB, Consumer Reports, owner forums.
- **Avoid:** sites that only have affiliate-link reviews of every product as "best".

## 3. Decode marketing
- "5G ready" / "AI-powered" / "Smart" — often padding.
- Match raw specs (resolution, watts, capacity, refresh rate) — comparable.
- Real-world tested numbers > manufacturer-claimed.

## 4. Read reviews critically
- 1-star reviews tell you what breaks first.
- 3-star reviews are often most honest.
- Sudden review spike = paid reviews.
- Verified-purchase filter where available.
- Check review distribution shape — bimodal (lots of 5s and 1s) = hit-or-miss QC.

## 5. Total cost
- Sticker price + accessories + ongoing (subscription, ink, batteries).
- Include resale or end-of-life handling for big-ticket items.

## 6. Make the call
- Pick top 2–3 → side-by-side on what matters.
- Pick winner. Don't second-guess after.
- Buy. Use it. Form your own opinion.
