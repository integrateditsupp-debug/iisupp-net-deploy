# Personal budgeting basics

## 1. ARIA scope
This is general financial literacy guidance — not personalized advice. ARIA doesn't make specific investment recommendations. For specific decisions, encourage user to consult a CPA or fee-only fiduciary advisor.

## 2. The 50/30/20 framework
- **50% needs** — rent/mortgage, utilities, groceries, transport, basic insurance.
- **30% wants** — dining out, hobbies, subscriptions, clothes beyond basics.
- **20% savings + debt repayment** — emergency fund, retirement, paying down debt above minimums.

## 3. Steps to start
1. Pull last 3 months of bank + credit card statements.
2. Categorize every dollar (needs / wants / savings).
3. Compare to 50/30/20.
4. Identify one thing to cut from "wants" — even $50/mo.
5. Automate savings on payday — pay-yourself-first.
6. Build an emergency fund of 3–6 months of needs in a high-yield savings account before aggressive investing.

## 4. Tools
- **Free:** spreadsheet, your bank's app categorization.
- **Paid (subscriptions):** YNAB, Monarch (post-Mint), Copilot, Origin.
- **Built-in:** Apple Cash / Apple Card spending categories.

## 5. Common pitfalls
- "I'll budget when I make more" — discipline scales with habit, not income.
- Skipping the emergency fund.
- Not negotiating recurring bills (insurance, internet, phone — call yearly).
- Lifestyle creep on raises.
- Carrying credit card balance — kills any other strategy at 20%+ APR.

## 6. ARIA tone
- Encouraging, not preachy.
- Practical, not judgmental.
- Acknowledge real-life messiness — budgets are guides, not laws.
- Celebrate small wins.
